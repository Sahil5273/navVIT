Academic Information and Procedures

F Grade
• Significance: An "F" grade signifies failure in a course. This can occur due to:
  1. Missing the exam.
  2. Scoring insufficient marks in the exam.
• Opportunities to Address an F Grade:
  1. Arrear Exam:
    • Conducted only in cases of medical emergencies for students who missed the exam.
  2. Supplementary Exam:
    • Conducted for all students who have failed a particular subject.
    • All students with an "F" grade are eligible for this opportunity.
• Important Note:
  • During supplementary, arrear, or grade improvement exams, re-evaluation requests are not entertained. Dissatisfaction or queries regarding the results cannot be addressed through re-evaluation
N Grade
• Significance: An "N" grade is awarded as a penalty for malpractice during the Term End Examination (TEE).
• Consequences of an N Grade:
  1. Denial of opportunity to write supplementary, arrear, or grade improvement exams for the affected course(s).
  2. If malpractice is proven, the "N" grade is assigned to all courses for that semester.
  3. The semester is declared as a semester back.
  4. Re-registration Requirement:
• Students must re-register for all courses in the semester where malpractice occurred.
• A re-registration fee of ₹6000 per course must be paid

VIT Bhopal Placement Guidelines

Students Planning for Higher Studies:
o Not eligible for Dream/Super Dream offers.
o They must submit proof of higher education applications
Students Placed in Dream/Super Dream Offers:
o Cannot register for other placements unless invited by the Placement Office.
o Registration allowed only for core/PSU companies.
Pre-Placement Internships:
o Students can attend internships during the final year based on approval.
o Internships are considered part of the academic program.
Multiple Offers:
o Students already placed in Dream or Super Dream offers cannot apply for other positions unless permitted by the Placement Office.
Eligibility for Students with Backlogs:
o Students with "N" or "F" grades are ineligible for placements.
o Once cleared, they can register for the next placement phase.
PAT Rules:
o Violating PAT norms, such as accepting multiple offers without approval, will result in disciplinary action.
o Students rejecting offers after accepting them will face strict action.

2+2 International Academic Program
The 2+2 Program offers students the unique opportunity to complete the first two years of their studies at VIT and the remaining two years at a foreign partner university of their choice. This program provides global exposure and the chance to earn an international degree.
Eligibility
  1. Minimum CGPA: Students must have a CGPA of 8.0 or above.
  2. Valid Passport: A valid passport is mandatory to participate in the program.
  3. Interest in Global Study: Students must express a willingness to study at one of the partner universities located in:
    • Asia
    • USA
    • Europe
    • Australia

    • Reasons for Dropping a Course:
    • Students facing difficulty in scheduling or experiencing any other unbearable challenges can request to drop a course.
    • Reporting Procedure:
    • Such cases must be immediately reported to the Program Chair (PC) of the respective branch within one week of registration.
    • Penalty for Late Reporting:
    • If a course drop is not reported within the stipulated time, a penalty of ₹6000 will be charged for the re-registration of the course in the next semester.

    Exam Related

    Feedback Form Requirement
    • Mandatory for All Students:
    • Filling out the Feedback Form is compulsory for all students before the Mid-Term or Term End Examination (TEE).
    • Consequences of Missing the Form:
    • Failure to complete the feedback form may result in the blocking of access to VTOP, which could hinder essential academic activities
    Exam Reappearance Policy
    Mid-Term Exam: • Reappearance Eligibility:
    • Students may reappear for a mid-term exam only in cases of a medical emergency.
    • Reporting Procedure:
    • The student must immediately inform the Proctor and Program Chair (PC) via email. Failure to report promptly may disqualify the student from reappearing.

    Term End Examination (TEE): • No Reappearance Option:
    • If a student misses the TEE due to any reason, including medical emergencies, the only available option is to appear for the Supplementary Exam.
    Procedures for Handling Malpractice Cases
    1. Immediate Action:
    If a student is caught engaging in malpractice during an exam:
    • The current paper is seized and used as evidence.
    • All remaining exams for the semester are immediately canceled.
    2. Submission to the Disciplinary Committee:
    • The confiscated paper, along with any other evidence, is submitted to the Disciplinary Board/Committee for review.
    • The Disciplinary Board/Committee comprises 4 to 5 members, who deliberate on the matter.
    3. Deliberation and Decision:
    • The committee thoroughly reviews the evidence and conducts discussions to determine if the malpractice claim is valid.
    • If the student is found guilty, the following actions are taken:
    1. An N Grade is assigned for all courses in that semester.
    2. The N Grade is reflected in the student’s score sheet.

    Hostel Contact Hierarchy and Guidelines
    1. First Point of Contact

      For the following issues, contact Hostel Supervisors:

      • Single Point Contact: For any general issue.

      • Leave/Outing Approvals.

      • Service and Maintenance Issues:
        • Electrical, Plumbing, Carpenter work, Wi-Fi, Housekeeping, etc.

      • Room-Related Issues: Submit an application to the Hostel Supervisors.

      • Mess-Related Issues.

      • Medical Emergency.


    2. Next Level of Contact

      If issues require further clarification, escalate to the Respective Block Wardens or Deputy Wardens as follows:

      • Leave-Related Clarification: Respective Block Wardens.

      • Room-Related Clarification: Respective Block Wardens.

      • Service and Maintenance Clarification: Respective Block Deputy Wardens.

      • Medical Emergency: Respective Wardens/Deputy Wardens.


    3. Final Level of Contact

      For unresolved issues, contact the Chief Warden:

      • Hostel-Related Issues: If not addressed at previous levels.

      • Vacating Procedures.

      • Discipline Issues: Contact the Chief Warden through the concerned Hostel Warden.
